Who This Is NOT For

If you're a Figma expert, Stitch won't replace your workflow:

  • Components with variants — Figma has size=sm/md/lg, state=default/hover/disabled. Stitch has none
  • Visual exploration speed — dragging beats prompting for rapid iteration
  • Design handoff — inspect mode, redlines, spacing overlays, asset export at 1x/2x/3x
  • Team governance — libraries, publishing, version history, branching, approvals
  • Prototyping — Figma prototypes with transitions and interactions for user testing

Figma is for designers designing. This talk isn't for you. (But stay — the MCP part is interesting.)

Talking Points (1.5 min)

This is the honest slide. Acknowledge Figma's strengths head-on. Don't position Stitch as a Figma replacement — it's not. The audience for this approach is the 90% of developers who ship UIs without Figma.
