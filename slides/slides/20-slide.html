Who This IS For

  • You're a backend engineer who needs a frontend
  • You don't have a designer (or your designer is overloaded)
  • You know what you want but can't express it in Figma
  • You've been pasting screenshots into ChatGPT and it mostly works

Stitch gets you 80% there — with a real design system, not just a page.
The last 20% you do in code, where you're comfortable.

For teams: MCP makes the design system shared infrastructure — tokens, sync, change detection, tombstoning. Multiple devs (and agents) collaborate on the same design system.

Talking Points (1.5 min)

This is the core thesis. For solo devs, screenshots are faster. But Stitch + MCP shines when you need a shared design system. The value isn't one screen — it's the system-level infrastructure: tokens everyone reads from, change detection that catches drift, bidirectional sync that keeps code and design aligned.
